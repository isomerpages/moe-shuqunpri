<h4><strong>Interested applicants must meet the following criteria:</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Singapore citizen or Singapore Permanent Resident (SPR) for stallholder and stall</li>
<li>Committed to providing good service and quality food with prices determined in consultation with the</li>
<li>Able to provide cooked food that meets the health / nutritional standards set by the Health Promotion Board (HPB) under the <a target="_blank" href="https://www.hpb.gov.sg/schools/school-programmes/healthy-meals-in-schools-programme">Healthy Meals in Schools Programme</a>. This includes providing a serving of fruits with the meal.</li>
<li>Able to maintain high standard of food safety and personal</li>
<li>Healthy and fit to operate the</li>
<li>Preferably possess some experience operating a food stall in a school environment</li>
</ul>
<h4><strong>Upon successful award, the stallholder is expected to:</strong></h4>
<ul>
<li>Possess the WSQ Basic Food Hygiene Certificate (SFA). Any stall assistant(s) will be required to possess the WSQ Basic Food Hygiene Certificate (SFA) too.</li>
<li>Apply for a food stall license from Singapore Food Agency (SFA).</li>
<li>Commence operation of the stall as soon as</li>
<li>Operate the stall based on the school-approved menu, from Monday to Friday during school term Should the need arise, the stallholder is expected to operate on selected days during school holidays.</li>
</ul>
<p>Shortlisted applicants will be notified for an interview and will be required to provide food sampling for the School Canteen Committee at the applicant’s own expense.</p>
